All About Hackers

When people think about the word "hacker", they may think of an evildoer who's able to gain unauthorized access to other computers, causing mayhem and damage for financial gain.

They might be surprised to learn that not all hackers are out to do harm, and they all have varying motivations for hacking.

Here, we've created a section detailing the three different categories of hackers and the various type of threat actors.

White hat hackers, also known as ethical hackers, are individuals who use their programming skills for good in a ethical and legal manner.

These hackers are given permission from companies or organizations to try and hack into their networks or computers.

They search for vulnerabilities or errors and report them before these vulnerabilities can be exploited and taken advantage of.

While some companies may contract or employ ethical hackers, they may also award prizes or bounties to ethical hackers that inform them of vulnerabilities.

Gray hat hackers are individuals who commit crimes and arguably unethical actions, but without malicious intent.

They are essentially the middle ground between white hat and black hat hackers.

For example, a gray hat hacker may compromise an organizaton's network or systems without its permission first.

Upon discovering vulnerabilities, the hacker may disclose them publicly or to the company afterwards.

Black hat hackers are unethical individuals who gain unauthorized access to networks or systems for malicious reasons or personal gain.

They seek to steal, modify, or delete data and their primary motivation is usually for financial gain, but could include espionage, protest, or simply the thrill of cyber crimes.

In any case, these hackers can inflict major damage on individual users and large organizations by stealing sensitive information, compromising security, or shutting down or altering services and functions.




Script Kiddies

Script kiddies, also known as amateurs, are individuals that have little to no skills in hacking.

Often times, they employ the use of existing tools, programs, and instructions found online in order to conduct attacks.

Most script kiddies are new or inexperienced hackers with varying motivations.

Some may just be curious, or wish to flaunt their skills by causing harm.

However, despite their lack of know-how, script kiddies should not be taken lightly, as they can cause devastating attacks.

Hacktivists

Hacktivists are hackers who protest against political and social issues by hacking for attention.

These attackers are typically politically or socially motivated and utilize the Internet to raise awareness and promote their message.

They may publicly protest against organizations or governments by publishing articles, videos, leaking sensitive information, and performing Distributed Denial of Service attacks.

One hacktivist group you may have heard about is Anonymous. While most groups aren't usually very organized, they can still cause serious disruptions to governments and businesses.

Hacktivists often rely on simple or freely available tools.

State-Sponsored

State-sponsored hackers are attackers who are funded and guided by a government.

These individuals are given orders to launch operations that a state wishes them to conduct.

The goal of their attacks can include gathering intelligence, sabotaging computer networks, stealing government secrets, interfering with internal politics, or committing industrial espionage.

The targets of state-sponsored hackers can range from governments to terrorist groups to corporations.

Many countries around the world participate to some degree in state-sponsored hacking.

Cyber Criminals

Cyber criminals are black hat hackers whose goal is to make money using any means possible.

While some are independent and work alone, others are funded and sponsored by criminal organizations.

They commit malevolent attacks on systems and networks in order to steal sensitive information or personal data, with the ultimate goal of generating profit.

Underground markets are used to trade malicious goods and services, like hacking tools or stolen information and specialize in certain products or services.

There are a large variety of victims that are targeted by cyber criminals, including businesses, consumers, and enterprises.
